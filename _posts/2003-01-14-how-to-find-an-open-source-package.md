How to Find an Open Source Package

This is an article I've been wanting to write for literally months now and today's little SquirrelMail snafu (curse Courier IMAP Auth daemon; curse it!) has given me a good reason.

**Note:** I know that both SquirrelMail and Courier IMAP are good Open Source projects and generally work fine.&nbsp; For me thought they blew up big time -- and right in a customer's face.&nbsp; That's bad.&nbsp; Yes it's my fault but it sent me questing for another webmail package FAST.&nbsp; Hence this article.

So here's the problem -- you need to find an Open Source application to do X where X is some kind of task either common or highly specific.&nbsp; You don't know where to start.&nbsp; Who you gonna call?&nbsp; SourceBusters<sup>1</sup>?&nbsp; Nope!&nbsp; But here's what you are going to do.

1. Figure out what you need. 
2. Figure out where to search. 
3. Figure out 

## Freshmeat, SourceForge and Google

There are three main tools that you can use to find an Open Source application:

- [Freshmeat](http://www.freshmeat.net/)
- [SourceForge](http://www.sf.net/)
- [Google](http://www.google.com/)

Each of these tools have unique pros and cons.&nbsp; Of the three I prefer Freshmeat but each is covered below.

### SourceForge

SourceForge is one of those required but bloody well annoying Internet things.&nbsp; Yes it is an absolute treasure trove of resources but it is clunky, difficult to search and just plain often doesn't work.&nbsp; Here's an example, when I went to do my webmail search on SF this is what I got:

But Sensei ... How Do I know If the Open Source is Good?

Ah... Well there is the rub.&nbsp; You often don't know.&nbsp; And when you just have a great big pile of bits all tarred together then it is difficult.&nbsp; But rest easy Grasshopper -- there are a number of ways to tell, you can put any Open Source package through one or more "trials" and see how it measures up to the task:

1. **The Trial of Self Acknowledgement**.
2. **The Trial of the Install**.
3. **The Trial of the Email**.&nbsp; Or "if an email is sent into the ether and no one hears it, can you get support?".
4. **The Trial of the Mailing List**.
5. **The Trial of the IM**.
